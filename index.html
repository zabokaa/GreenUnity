Short History

GreenUnity, established in 2011, began its roots as a community garden and learning space on an abandoned plot in the heart of the city. Spanning nearly 6000 square meters, the garden featured numerous raised-beds, open workshops, a learning kitchen for processing local harvests, and space for markets, workshops, and networking events for individuals passionate about participatory and sustainable urban design. Initially conceived as a mobile urban garden, the organization behind GreenUnity, Nomadic Green, shifted its activities to a new location. The new site, situated on partial grounds of the New St. Jacobi Cemetery, encompasses 7.5 hectares of natural space. Embracing a collaborative approach aligned with the needs of all users, GreenUnity strives to nurture the garden collectively, contributing to the preservation of this publicly accessible green area. GreenUnity stands as a diverse project with a broad organizational structure and various areas of action. Operating with ecological practices using heirloom seeds, the project emphasizes the importance of old, rare varieties, and biodiversity. Always welcoming new participants, GreenUnity engages in funding projects that address important themes, such as inclusion and adapting to the everyday impacts of the climate crisis. In the realm of participatory horticulture, the project designs gardens for individuals in their locations and neighborhoods, not only establishing the garden but also providing professional and educational support for new initiatives.

Our Vision + Mision

MORE JUSTICE

Whether it's about education or good food, many people lack access. For some, it's harder than for others, and for some, it's impossible without help. We want to create opportunities beyond monetary connections.

MORE TOGETHER

Because no one can do everything and good ideas need space, we share the space with others. We see ourselves as a neighborhood platform and also as part of a network from the global to the local level that shares our goals.

MORE PERSPECTIVE

Social and ecological crises are the issues of our time. Not only changing climate has catastrophic consequences. Isolation and populism cannot be answers to global challenges. We work intensively on the topics of societal transformation, the future of the city, and firmly believe that 'valuable' can mean more than 'money!'
